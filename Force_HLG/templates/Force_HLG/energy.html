The Sledding Problem

Anna’s favorite winter sport is sledding. This year, she is attending a sledding competition. Anna wants to estimate her speed at the bottom of the hill. Given her mass (Anna + the sled) is \(\mathbf{m}\), the height of the hill is \(\mathbf{h_0}\), energy loss due to friction between the sled and the snow is \(\mathbf{W}\), what is Anna’s speed \(\color{red} \mathbf{V_f}\) at the bottom of the hill? (\(\mathbf{g}\) is the gravitational constant)

Step 1: What are types of energy and their formula that are involved in this problem?

1. Potential energy \(\mathbf{=mgh_0}\)

1. Potention energy \(\mathbf{=mgh}\)
2. Kinetic energy \(\mathbf{=\frac{1}{2}mv^2}\)
3. Energy loss due to friction \(\mathbf{=W}\)

1. Kinetic energy \(\mathbf{\frac{1}{2}mV_{f}^{2}}\)
